FCRA Sections to Support Your Disputes

  • Section 611 (15 U.S.C. § 1681i) : Requires credit reporting agencies to investigate disputes and correct or delete inaccurate information within 30 days.

    • Use this section when disputing with credit bureaus.

  • Section 623 (15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2) : Requires furnishers (creditors) to report accurate information and correct any inaccuracies.

    • Use this section when disputing directly with creditors.

  • Section 607 (15 U.S.C. § 1681e) : Requires credit reporting agencies to follow reasonable procedures to ensure maximum possible accuracy of the information in consumer reports.

    • Can be used in both credit bureau and creditor disputes.

  • Section 604 (15 U.S.C. § 1681b) : Specifies permissible purposes for which a consumer reporting agency may furnish a consumer report.

    • Use this section when disputing unauthorized inquiries.

Legal Actions: Filing for Declaratory Relief

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Identify the Loan Servicer :

    • Find out who is servicing your student loan or handling the repossession. This is typically found on your billing statement or credit report.

  2. Demand Debt Verification :

    • Write a letter to the loan servicer demanding they verify the debt within 30 days. According to the FCRA (15 U.S.C. § 1681g), you have the right to request and obtain all the information about you in the files of a consumer reporting agency.

  3. File a Complaint for Declaratory Relief :

    • If the servicer cannot provide proper verification, file a Complaint for Declaratory Relief in your local circuit court. This complaint asks the court to declare that you do not owe any money to the servicer.

  4. Wait for Response :

    • The lender has 30 days to respond to the complaint. If they do not respond, you win by default. The FCRA mandates that consumer reporting agencies must correct or delete inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information within 30 days (15 U.S.C. § 1681i).

  5. File a Motion for Default Judgment :

    • After 30 days, if the lender has not responded, file a Motion for Default Judgment. This motion asks the court to officially declare that you have no valid debt to the lender.

  6. Court Declaration :

    • The court will enter an order declaring that you owe nothing to the lender. This means you no longer have to make payments on the loan.

Establishing business credit is a crucial step for any new business, including a luxury car rental business. Here’s a detailed guide to help you build and manage your business credit effectively:

Step-by-Step Guide to Establishing Business Credit

Step 1: Set Up Your Business Properly

  1. Form a Legal Business Entity :

    • Register your business as an LLC or corporation. This separates your personal and business finances, protecting your personal assets.

  2. Obtain an EIN :

    • Apply for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS. This functions like a Social Security Number for your business and is necessary for tax purposes and opening a business bank account.

  3. Open a Business Bank Account :

    • Use your EIN to open a business checking account. Keep all business finances separate from your personal accounts.

  4. Get a Business Phone Number and Address :

    • Register a dedicated business phone number and address. You can use a virtual address or PO Box if you don’t have a physical office.

  5. Register with Dun &Bradstreet :

    • Apply for a D-U-N-S Number from Dun &Bradstreet (D &B). This is a unique identifier for your business and is used by lenders and suppliers to check your creditworthiness.

Step 2: Establish Trade Lines

  1. Start with Vendor Credit :

    • Work with vendors that report to business credit bureaus. Purchase supplies or services and pay on time.
    • Examples of vendors include Uline, Grainger, and Quill.

  2. Apply for a Business Credit Card :

    • Choose a business credit card that reports to business credit bureaus. Options include the Chase Ink Business Cash, Amex Blue Business Cash, and Capital One Spark.

  3. Use Credit Responsibly :

    • Use your business credit card for regular expenses and pay off the balance in full each month to build a positive payment history.

Step 3: Monitor Your Business Credit

  1. Check Your Business Credit Reports :

    • Regularly check your business credit reports from D &B, Experian Business, and Equifax Business. This helps you track your progress and identify any errors.

  2. Dispute Errors :

    • If you find any inaccuracies in your business credit reports, dispute them with the respective bureau to have them corrected.

Step 4: Build Strong Credit Habits

  1. Pay Bills on Time :

    • Always pay your bills and invoices on time. Late payments can negatively impact your business credit score.

  2. Manage Credit Utilization :

    • Keep your credit utilization ratio (the amount of credit you’re using compared to your total credit limit) below 30%.

  3. Limit Credit Inquiries :

    • Avoid applying for too many credit accounts in a short period, as this can result in multiple hard inquiries on your credit report.

Step 5: Expand Your Credit Profile

  1. Apply for a Business Line of Credit :

    • Once you’ve established a good credit history, apply for a business line of credit. This provides flexible financing for your business needs.

  2. Consider Business Loans :

    • As your business grows, consider applying for business loans to finance larger purchases or expansion plans.
